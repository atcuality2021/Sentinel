Sentinel is not a chatbot wrapper. It is a research platform: a planner-driven multi-agent system that turns one question into a structured, cited, audience-tailored deliverable — and can do so with provable data sovereignty (zero cloud egress) when a customer's compliance posture demands it. This document maps the product's real capabilities to three commercial questions — how it makes money, how it goes to market, and how it benefits consumers (B2C) — then lays out the roadmap to close the gaps.

01What Sentinel actually does today

Every claim below is grounded in shipped code (835 passing tests). The pattern for any research run is: plan → research (web + KB + private connectors) → [optional extract] → synthesize → grade → render-for-persona. The output is never a wall of prose — it is a typed artifact with sources, a public/private boundary tag on every fact, gaps that were left unfilled, and a prioritized action plan.

Multi-domain engine

9 research domains, each with its own typed deliverable schema — market battlecards, account briefs, software/library evaluations, literature surveys, financial profiles, nutrition briefs, travel guides, government proposals, and product comparisons.

Audience personas

6 built-in + unlimited custom. A persona changes reading level, tone, format, and source policy — not the facts. The same research renders as a K-12 study guide or a CFO brief. Auto-selected by domain.

Sovereign by design

Three compliance modes. In on_prem_required the cloud model is never even constructed — provable by code introspection. Every source is tagged public or private.

Dual-tier cost control

Small 12B model does tool-calling; large 26B does reasoning only. Capability-based routing cuts reasoning cost materially and is admin-tunable in config — no redeploy.

Knowledge base + memory

Per-project KB (hybrid BM25 + vector + rerank), auto-ingestion of every artifact, and episodic memory that answers "what changed since last time" across runs.

Trust scaffolding

Deterministic citation grading (no LLM) hard-blocks artifacts with unresolved sources. High-stakes domains (medical, legal) are refused at task creation. An autonomy gate keeps a human in the loop by default.

The one-line thesis Everyone is building "AI search." Sentinel's wedge is sovereign-to-action: cited, audience-shaped, memory-aware research that a regulated buyer can run with zero data leaving their perimeter — and that same engine, wrapped for consumers, becomes a trustworthy personal research assistant that shows its sources.

04Monetization — five revenue models

These are not alternatives to choose between — they are a sequenced portfolio. Lead with the highest-margin, lowest-CAC motion (sovereign enterprise), use it to fund the consumer build-out.

① Sovereign Enterprise License  B2B · primary revenue

On-prem / VPC-deployed Sentinel for regulated organizations — banks, hospitals, government, defense contractors. They pay for the one thing no cloud competitor can credibly offer: research-grade AI where data provably never leaves the perimeter, with an audit log and provenance on every fact.

  • Model: annual platform license + per-seat or per-deployment, plus an implementation/onboarding fee.
  • Anchor: the govt_proposal and account domains are tailor-made for this buyer.
  • Margin: highest. Software license against a compliance budget, not a tooling budget.

② Managed Cloud SaaS (tiered)  B2B / prosumer

Hosted multi-tenant Sentinel for teams that don't need on-prem — agencies, consultancies, startups, sales teams. Tiered by seats, run volume, and feature depth (memory, KB size, custom personas, connectors).

③ Consumer Subscription  B2C

A prosumer app for the four consumer domains. The pitch: "Perplexity that remembers you, tailors to your level, and never bluffs a source." Freemium with a metered free tier, paid for unlimited runs, deeper research mode, and persistent personal memory/KB.

④ Usage Credits & Domain Packs  Both

Pay-as-you-go research credits for occasional users (one student writing one paper; one shopper before one big purchase). "Domain packs" let a buyer unlock a vertical (e.g., a Travel pack, a Job-hunt pack) without a full subscription — a low-friction first dollar.

⑤ Platform / API & White-label  B2B

The "bring your own domain" extensibility (a domain is a prompt + an output schema, no code change) is a real platform play. Sell API access and white-label the engine to vertical SaaS companies who want cited, sovereign research inside their product (a fintech embedding financial profiles; an EdTech embedding literature surveys).

Sequencing Now → 12 mo: sovereign enterprise + managed SaaS (revenue + reference logos).   6 → 18 mo: consumer subscription + credit packs (funnel + brand).   12 mo+: API / white-label (leverage). The enterprise margin pays for the consumer CAC.

06B2C: the benefit, and the honest gap

Why consumers would actually want this

The consumer value proposition is sharper than "another AI search box," because the engine already does three things generic chat assistants do badly:

It shows its work

Every claim is tied to a source with a URL. Gaps are stated, not bluffed. For a student citing a paper or a shopper trusting a recommendation, verifiability is the product.

It speaks your level

The same answer renders as a K-12 study guide, a developer's comparison table, or a plain-language buying recommendation — persona-tailored, auto-selected by topic.

It remembers you

A personal KB + episodic memory means your travel research, your reading list, your shortlist build up over time instead of evaporating after each chat.

Concrete consumer journeys the engine supports today

  • The student — "Summarize the state of research on X with sources" → an academic literature survey rendered as a study guide with definitions and worked examples.
  • The shopper — "Best ___ under ₹___" → a product_research comparison with specs, value ranking, and a named winner.
  • The traveler — "Plan a budget trip to ___" → a travel brief with visa, season, safety, and budget, saved into a trip project.
  • The developer — "Should I adopt ___?" → a software evaluation of stack, community, and licensing as a comparison table.
  • The retail investor — "Is ___ worth it?" → a finance profile with a clearly-labeled thesis and risk signals (with disclaimers).
The honest gap — read before promising a B2C launch The codebase audit is unambiguous: today Sentinel is architected as a single-tenant, single-password internal tool. There is no multi-user account system, no signup flow, no billing/payments, no per-user rate limiting or usage metering, and no data isolation between users. The research engine is consumer-grade; the account & commerce layer around it is not built yet. A B2C launch is a product-build, not a marketing decision — see the roadmap (§08).

The good news: the mobile-friendly UI just shipped (responsive off-canvas navigation, phone breakpoints), so the front door is consumer-ready. What remains is the account/billing/tenancy spine.

07Marketing & go-to-market

Positioning

Category & one-liner Category: Sovereign AI research platform.   Enterprise line: "Research-grade AI that proves your data never left the building."   Consumer line: "The research assistant that shows its sources, speaks your language, and remembers."

The wedge (don't fight the red ocean)

Generic competitor/account intelligence is a saturated market. Sentinel should not lead with "we do competitor research too." Lead with the two things rivals structurally can't match: provable sovereignty (for B2B) and cited, level-tailored, memory-aware answers (for B2C). Win the wedge, then expand across domains.

Growth loops

  • Shareable cited reports. Every consumer export carries a "researched with Sentinel — see sources" footer and an optional public link. Sourced answers get shared (and screenshotted) precisely because they're trustworthy — built-in distribution.
  • SEO from the engine. Publish a curated stream of public product_research / software / travel reports. They rank for high-intent long-tail queries and funnel to the app.
  • Enterprise → consumer halo. "The sovereign engine trusted by [regulated logo] now in your pocket" is a credibility transfer few consumer AI apps can claim.
  • Persona / domain virality. Custom personas and (future) shared domain packs are inherently shareable artifacts — "I made a 'CFO brief' persona, here it is."

Content engine

Use Sentinel to market Sentinel: the product literally produces publishable, cited research. A weekly "sourced explainer" in each consumer domain doubles as SEO inventory and a live capability demo.

09Risks & honest limitations

  • Consumer category is brutal. Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini own mindshare and have free distribution. Sentinel must win a specific wedge (sourced + sovereign + memory), not compete head-on.
  • The B2C build is real work. The commerce/tenancy spine (§08 Track A P0) is months of engineering; do not market a consumer launch before it exists.
  • Sovereign deployments carry services weight. On-prem sales close slowly and need implementation muscle — high margin but high touch. Staff for it.
  • Cost discipline. Without per-user metering, a freemium consumer tier can bleed cloud spend. Rate limits and the on-prem/12B routing are the cost defense — ship them before opening the funnel.
  • Trust is binary. One widely-shared hallucinated citation damages the whole "shows its sources" brand. The deterministic grader + URL-reachability check (P1) are reputational insurance, not polish.
  • Regulatory scope. Clinical/legal are deliberately gated. Don't unlock them for TAM before the eval + allow-list safety infrastructure is real — the liability is asymmetric.
